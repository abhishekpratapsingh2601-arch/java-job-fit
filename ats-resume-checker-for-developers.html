Free ATS Resume Checker for Developers

Most companies run resumes through an applicant tracking system (ATS) before a human reads them. The ATS ranks how well your resume matches the job description, mostly by keywords and required skills. If your resume does not echo the language of the posting, a strong developer can be filtered out automatically. JavaJobFit is a free ATS resume checker that shows you that match the way the system sees it — a score, your matched strengths, and the exact keywords you are missing — so you can fix the gaps before you apply.

What an ATS actually checks

  • Required hard skills and tools named in the job description.
  • Exact keyword and phrase matches (frameworks, languages, databases).
  • Clear section structure and standard headings it can parse.
  • Job titles and experience signals relevant to the role.

How to pass the ATS without gaming it

Passing the ATS is not about tricks like white hidden text — modern systems ignore or flag those. It is about honestly mirroring the job description: if the role requires "REST APIs" and "SQL" and you have done both, use those exact words inside real achievement bullets. The checker shows which required terms are missing so you add only the ones you can truthfully back up.

Why a developer-specific checker helps

Generic resume tools do not understand that "Spring Boot," "JPA," "JUnit," and "CI/CD" are the signals that matter for backend roles. This checker is tuned for software and Java/Spring Boot developers, so the matched strengths and missing keywords are technical and actionable, not vague.
